Methods

What this is

A bottom-up study of transit-survey selection effects. We procedurally generate the full spectrum of plausible stellar systems consistent with the laws of physics and current occurrence statistics, insert validated planets, simulate their light signatures with astrophysical (stellar) noise, and score detectability for real and future observatories. Every object carries typed plausibility flags; draws that violate physics are rejected and never enter the database.

Generation physics

  • Stars: Kroupa (2001) IMF 0.08–2.2 M☉; main-sequence scaling relations; Stefan–Boltzmann Teff; thin-disk metallicities; Flower/Torres bolometric corrections; Claret-like limb darkening.
  • Stellar noise: granulation via νmax scaling (Kallinger+ 2014), p-modes (Kjeldsen & Bedding 1995), rotational spot variability with gyrochronology (Skumanich; McQuillan+ 2014 amplitudes), M-dwarf flares; chromatic spot factors per band (Rackham+ 2018).
  • Planets: Kepler-shaped occurrence (Petigura+ 2013 periods, Fulton+ 2017 radius valley), metallicity-scaled giants (Fischer & Valenti 2005), probabilistic mass–radius (Chen & Kipping 2017 style), Kipping (2013) eccentricities, Hill-stability-checked assembly (Gladman 1993, Pu & Wu 2015).
  • Atmospheres: class from bulk properties + cosmic shoreline (Zahnle & Catling 2017); scale heights; random cloud suppression; TSM/ESM (Kempton+ 2018); JWST feature detectability with stellar-contamination floors.
  • Transits: Winn (2010) geometry; numerically exact quadratic limb-darkened light curves.

Observatories

TESS, Kepler (archival), JWST NIRISS SOSS & NIRSpec Prism, a ground 1-m survey, and two future facilities whose specs will evolve: the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope Galactic Bulge Time Domain Survey (launch late 2026; modeled as "if this system were in a Roman field") and the Habitable Worlds Observatory (2040s, pre-Phase-A; reflected light direct imaging with ~1e-10 contrast and ~60 mas inner working angle). Full assumptions, sources, and volatility notes: docs/OBSERVATORIES.md in the repository.

Validation

The generator is checked against real confirmed exoplanets (NASA Exoplanet Archive TAP when available; The Extrasolar Planets Encyclopaedia as fallback — provenance recorded). Two checks: (1) real planets are run through our hard physics rules — violations are catalog measurement artifacts at the ~1–4% level, dominated by transiting brown dwarfs and low-SNR masses; (2) selection-matched population comparison via two-sample KS tests. See the Validation page.
